    • Saint Mary's Lake

    We packed our bags and headed to the mountains to get married. We hiked to this beautiful lake in the mountains of East Glacier National Park to exchange vows with our daughter by our side. This is Saint Mary's Lake, the second largest lake in the park. The lake is tucked away between the majestic, steeply rising mountains on all sides. The trees stood tall with wild flowers bordering the banks that reflected off the crystal clear waters.





    Polebridge

    Although the mountains made for a beautiful wedding, there are so many other reasons to visit this beautiful state. After our wedding we explored as much as we could for the duration of our stay. I would highly recommend going in the summer or fall for perfect weather. We went in June, and experienced flooding that closed a major section of the park causing us to change dates and locations for the wedding. There are still so many other beautiful places away from the main attractions. Here we hiked the Bowman trail. We were a few miles from the Canadian border and it was very difficult to get to. Polebridge was the only place for miles, tucked away in the middle of the mountains. There is tons of wildlife from elk, moose, and bears roaming freely in the praries. If you visit bring your bear spray! This hike was only a few miles through heavily wooded areas, crossing rushing rivers, and opening to this beautiful meadow full of wildflowers.





    Bowman Lake

    After leaving Polebridge we couldn't help to go deeper in to the mountains. Driving up gravel roads that hugged the side of the mountains until we finally came to a trail head. This lake was the deepest we could get in the park. We were in the middle of nowhere, with no other hikers, and no service yet we trecked along. We hiked straight up till the trail platued to this opening. Walking through thick trees that stood so high you couldn't see the sky. Then this lake just appeared with mountains on every side and crystal clear water. This was the most beautiful place I had ever seen. The reward of seeing this place definatly outweighed the long difficult journey to get there.





    East Glacier

    This was the best experience of my life. Although nothing went as planned, everything turned out perfect. When you're in a place like Glacier National Park, nothing could go wrong. After our ceremony, we hiked as high as we could to get a look over the mountains and take it all in. It was just the two of us, our daughter, and our photographer. We popped a bottle of champaign and watched the sun fall over the mountains changing the blue skies to shades of blue and purple. This is a moment that I will never forget. I hope sharing my experience motivates you to visit, and let the experience change you like it did me.







    Jaclyn McElroy-Thompson 2022
